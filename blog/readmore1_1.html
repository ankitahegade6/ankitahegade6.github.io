Flowers

Title description, May 28, 2020

The Holy Bible says God created all trees, plants, fruits, and flowers according to their various kinds - Genesis 1 : 1-13. Yes, God created flowers for us to know that He exists. Flowers are the sweetest things God ever made. Every flower is a soul blossoming out to Nature. Flowers may beckon towards us, but they speak towards Heaven and God. The flower is an assertion of man's eternal yearning for faith in the Ultimate.

The Philosophy.

One day Lord Buddha brought a flower near His eyes and left the scene. People could not understand what He meant . His disciple, Mahakashyap, could understand what Buddha told in silence ! - the Philosophy of flower, what it teaches mankind. The flower decorates itself with other flowers in the tree , causing no harm to other flowers and the tree, which bears it. It makes the environment beautiful. We, like the flowers should live harmoniously without causing harm to other living beings to make God's creations beautiful. Further, in a flower, the petals are harmoniously arranged along with the stem and other parts to make the flower beautiful. Similarly, we should keep up an internal harmony, by balancing our activity, rather activity of our body, and mind. Lastly the flower is making the nature beautiful for a good cause. However, the philosophy of anything beautiful should have to be realised and learned.

Purpose of creation of flowers.

God created flowers to beautify man's life. John Gerard, English Jesuit priest, says : " Flowers bring to a liberall and gentlemanly minde the remembrance of honostie, comelinesse and all kinds of virtues " . God created flowers for a definite purpose -- vamsa vardhana . Everybody loves pretty flowers .. even insects and birds. Flowers are just nice to look at, they also have an important function. Flowers help all kinds of plants reproduce by making seeds that will grow up into new plants. Birds and insects need flowers to get honey and pollen and flowers need insects and birds for pollination and seed dispersal. And, where flowers bloom so does hope.
